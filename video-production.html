Looking for a crisp cut? Video editing takes attention to detail, pacing, and just a little bit of an X-factor. Turn your footage into the best version of itself with Studio O’Riley.

Editing

I am trained in a number of video editing platforms, including Adobe Premiere, Davinci Resolve, and Final Cut. Each video gets the attention it deserves and with my Complete Package Delivery Policy, you retain the right to the edit file, not just the final product.

Color Correction

Davinci Resolve offers the latest in color grade technology, offering node-based correction and mask tracking for pinpoint fine-tuning. As it’s my preferred editing platform, you’ll get a top notch touch up.

Motion Graphics

Studio O'Riley motion graphics are powered by Adobe After Effects and DaVinci Resolve Fusion. With the right plugin, you can build anything and make it do anything. I consider it my job to take your graphics to the next level with careful detail and smooth transitions.

Compositing

Green screens, masks, stock footage, the world of cinema has it all. Compositing is about tricking the eye, and doing it well. Need to replace your background? Add dark sky and snow to a bright summer meadow? Using rotoscoping, motion tracking, and a whole lot of technical mumbo jumbo, Studio O’Riley will transform your scene into… a different scene.

Audio

Ever been totally captivated by an epic video, only to crash back to Earth because of poor audio quality? Audio will make or break a scene every time. Whether you need music, voice over, or the perfect sound effect, we’ll work together to shape your audio landscape to fit your needs.

Voice Over

I like to talk. I like it even better in front of a microphone. If you need a voice for your video, I’m right here.

Subtitles

Subtitles are important because they’re all about accessibility and going the extra mile for your consumers. In fact, some platforms and channels require your content to include subtitles for exactly that reason. Studio O'Riley goes the extra mile, making sure your subtitles fit the style of your video and that they don't interfere with other graphics on the screen.
